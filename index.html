The Strategy

Four engines feeding one ecosystem. I’m building the software of tomorrow, creating the best tech content I can today and documenting the whole journey in real-time.

My name is Lyubomir, I grew up in a small town in Bulgaria, left home at 14 for a math high school, and moved to Germany at 19. After studying CS at TU Munich and spending six years there, I joined Yelp in Hamburg, eventually becoming Staff Software Engineer.

2. Portfolio of Apps

Product Engine

Focus: Shipping multiple products & Rapid Iteration.

The Strategy: I am building a portfolio of apps. This allows me to test different ideas, diversify my risk, and find what truly resonates with users.

The Goal: To ship consistently and build a suite of useful tools, using each launch to learn and refine my development skills.

3. Streaming

Accountability Engine

Focus: Deep Work & Life Logging. A community for high-performer builders, workers, and students.

The Why: Accountability & Memories. It forces consistency, but it's also a digital time capsule. I want to look back years from now and remember that this journey was hella fun.

The Vision: Focused work will always be the "main menu." However, I am eventually expanding into a "Life Stream" once I reach Level 3 - broadcasting morning routines, workouts, and seaside walks. The goal is a 24/7 window into the full lifestyle, not just the grind.

The Monetization Model

Once we reach 1,000 subscribers, I will use YouTube's full monetization features (Ads & Memberships) for two reasons:

  1. Ramen Profitability: I'd be super happy if this channel covers my basic expenses so I can sustain the journey indefinitely without worrying about money.
  2. The Algorithm: To signal YouTube to push the channel forward. Without this signal, the channel dies because it doesn't make money for the platform.

However, Focus comes first.
I will manually trigger ads only at the start of breaks to ensure the sessions are 100% uninterrupted. YouTube is a bit unpredictable, but I'll do my best to keep the Ad breaks short (max 60s).

A Note for Students: If you are a student on a tight budget - I was there once too. Your presence alone is more than appreciated. Please save your money and invest it in your education! Only support the stream if you have disposable income to spare.
